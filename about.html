Company Overview

At Humanitarian Arts, we believe in the adage that if you give someone a fish, you feed them for a day; if you teach a person to fish, they will eat for a lifetime. Humanitarian Arts is a platform for everyone to go fishing. We help artisans in developing countries work their way out of poverty by providing a way for them to directly sell their work online and through a network of affiliates. Affiliates are responsible for marketing products online and through local pop-up gallery events. Pop-up gallery events allow people to experience unique works of art and discover affordable everyday products made with natural and recycled materials. Pop-up galleries can last for a few hours or even days and can be in someone's home, in an event space, or even in a retail store.

We encourage artisans to preserve their local culture through the art and everyday products they produce. We also help them create new products and re-engineer existing products to be more marketable, profitable, and environmentally sustainable. We encourage artisans to reinvest their business profits to improve the quality of their products and the safety and profitability of their business. We also work with artisans to reduce the environmental impact of their business by connecting them with sustainable and known sources of materials and encouraging them to incorporate recycled materials into their products.

Vision and Mission

Humanitarian Arts creates the opportunity for someone to purchase a fantastic product that provides a hand up and not a handout to hardworking, skilled artisans in developing countries. Humanitarian Arts connects buyers and sellers through our eCommerce platform that integrates our supply chain management technology with a multi-channel sales network to bridge the gap between people in the connected world and the often-unconnected world of people in developing countries.

Community Investment

We invest in the communities that artisans live in by using at least 10% of net profits to develop creative solutions to community and environmental challenges identified by local artisans. For example, Humanitarian Arts reinvests profits to replant the trees used in wood products, which also offset the carbon emissions of transporting products to customers. We also invest in science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics (STEAM) educational programs to improve their children's future. We believe that by working together positively and transparently, we can improve the environment and make everyone's daily life a little more enjoyable.

Our Process and Technology

The Humanitarian Arts platform is an intelligent system that combines state-of-the-art technology and data science with practical solutions to connect artisans in developing markets to a global marketplace. The key to the Humanitarian Arts platform is a smart product card (hCard) that solves the challenge of connecting artisans without Internet access to our eCommerce platform and managing our supply chain. The hCard combines product and artisan information to become a certificate of authenticity for the product and a business card for the artisan. The hCard also creates a delightful experience for customers because they can use it to learn about their purchase and follow their favorite artisan's progress on humanitarianarts.com.

The hCard communicates to artisans what products to make and ensures that they receive payment for each item they create. Local Humanitarian Arts agents (hAgents) use a back-office web-based application to print hCards and distribute them to each artisan when they pick up products. When a local hAgent picks up a product from an artisan, they perform a quality inspection and scan the hCard to acknowledge pickup. When an hCard is accepted, the artisan is paid a downpayment for their item through mobile money or bank transfer. Local agents do not carry cash with them to ensure their safety and the safety of the artisans. hAgents return to their office to check-in and pack products for bulk shipment to the Humanitarian Arts warehouse, where we sell them through the Humanitarian Arts website and affiliate network.

When a shipment of products arrives at the Humanitarian Arts central office, we examine each product and scan the hCard to record receiving the product and make it available for sale. At the point of purchase, we scan the hCard, and the artisan receives the balance due. Humanitarian Arts incorporate the data from the entire transaction process into our sales and supply chain management algorithm to improve our process and determine what new hCards to distribute.

History

Humanitarian Arts was created in 2020 by our founder Keith Williams to help the people in a Zambian village adapt to the loss of revenue due to the COVID pandemic. Keith came to Zambia as a consultant to teach software engineering to Zambian government employees. He had been to the village on prior visits and planned to buy some souvenirs to take back to the USA. However, on this visit to the village, Keith could see that they were suffering from the devastating loss of income from the lack of income and hope for the people. He realized that he had to use his vast knowledge of software and skills as an entrepreneur to solve the problem. Keith initially started buying items to take back to the US to sell; however, it quickly became apparent that the people needed hope and a long term solution. So, he worked with the village to understand their unique challenges and develop the concept and implementation of Humanitarian Arts.
